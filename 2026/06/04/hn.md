## 8. [Gaussian Point Splatting](https://momentsingraphics.de/Siggraph2026.html)

**168 points** | [64 comments](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48396792)

> **Article:** Gaussian Point Splatting is a rendering technique that addresses scalability challenges in Gaussian splatting by sampling pixel-sized points from Gaussians and using GPU atomics to splat them efficiently. Its key contributions include an even distribution of workload across millions of threads and solving the problem of determining how many points to splat while maintaining faithfulness to the original approach, enabling real-time rendering of hundreds of millions of Gaussians with only minor visual differences.
>
> **Discussion:** The comments reveal a split consensus: while Gaussian Splatting shows promise for real-world scene capture and VR training data, skeptics argue it will not replace traditional rendering in AAA games due to performance bottlenecks, physics/shadow difficulties, and rendering artifacts. The technical discussion centers on whether this is a novel contribution or a refinement of established 1990s point-rendering techniques now made practical through modern GPU architecture.
