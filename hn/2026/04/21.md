## 2. [All phones sold in the EU to have replaceable batteries from 2027](https://www.theolivepress.es/spain-news/2026/04/20/eu-to-force-replaceable-batteries-in-phones-and-tablets-from-2027/) (1408 pts, [1208 comments](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47834195))

## 3. [GitHub's fake star economy](https://awesomeagents.ai/news/github-fake-stars-investigation/) (790 pts, [367 comments](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47831621))

**Article:** An investigation building on a peer-reviewed CMU study reveals that approximately 6 million fake GitHub stars have been distributed across 18,617 repositories by around 301,000 accounts, with stars selling for as little as $0.03-$0.85 each. AI/LLM repositories are the largest non-malicious category of fake-star recipients, and the problem accelerated dramatically in 2024, with 16.66% of repos with 50+ stars involved in fake star campaigns. **Discussion:** Commenters express deep skepticism about GitHub stars as a quality metric, invoking Goodhart's Law to argue that once stars became a target for VC evaluation, they ceased to be useful indicators. Many argue that meaningful project evaluation requires examining commit history, issue handling, and code quality rather than vanity metrics.
