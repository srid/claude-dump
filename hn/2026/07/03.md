**Article:** The article argues that Switzerland's fast, cheap fiber internet (up to 25 Gbit symmetric) results not from a laissez-faire market but from treating fiber as a natural monopoly: infrastructure is built once as a shared, neutral asset with mandated point-to-point architecture and open access, letting providers compete on services over the same lines. **Discussion:** A major thread disputes the article's omission of population density, with some arguing Switzerland's higher density explains cheaper deployment and others countering that dense US cities like New York still lag, making regulation the real culprit. Others questioned the speed claims by pointing to Speedtest global-index data, prompting discussion of sampling bias. A long tangent explored the marketing premium of "Swiss" branding and "Made in"/"Designed in" labels across countries.
