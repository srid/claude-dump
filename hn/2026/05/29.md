### 13. [Disagreement among frontier LLMs on real-world fact-checks](https://lenz.io/research/llm-disagreement) — [501 comments](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48307887)

**Article:** Research from Lenz tested five frontier AI models (including GPT-5.4, Claude Opus 4.7, and Gemini 3 Pro) on 1,000 real-world user-submitted claims and found that models disagreed on 67% of them, with a Krippendorff's alpha of 0.639 indicating limited agreement. Middle-ground verdicts like "Mostly True" and "Misleading" showed the weakest consensus, and even retrieval-augmented models with web search disagreed 42% of the time. The researchers concluded that relying on any single model inherits that disagreement and announced plans for a follow-up study with human-labeled ground truth.

**Discussion:** The dominant criticism focused on flawed methodology -- critics argued the study forced models into binary choices without an "I don't know" option, artificially inflating disagreement, and that the four-bucket classification system lacked clear definitions. Multiple commenters called for a human baseline comparison, noting that human fact-checkers performing identical tasks might disagree at similar rates. Others highlighted that better prompts with explicit rubrics can dramatically improve model agreement, and that Claude Opus's tendency to hedge (45% middle-category usage) versus Gemini's confidence (6% hedging) reflects different design philosophies rather than pure disagreement.
